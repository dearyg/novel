## The Core Idea

The idea is basically there's only one soul in the world, only one person in a sense, that everyone just keeps their own memory but the actual soul is the same. So this is very scary in a sense that our soul just simultaneously feels different persons — think about it, the other person you hate actually is you just with different memory.

## The Plot Structure

The plot's main character is a person who slowly realizes this with some abilities. Start of the novel narrative asks the question: who you are, why we have a soul, why we can feel, etc. And over different chapters the main character slowly realizes the truth and in the end reveals it to the world.

## Key Sectors / Themes

- Wars, fighting with other people, making others suffer
- Main characters can be: 流浪汉 (homeless person), boss, friends, family
- Maybe an old person who represents so-called God but turns out to be just yourself
- Many people believe in 宗教 (religion) because of fear of death and hoping for afterlife — and people ruling and controlling through it

## The Learning Curve

1. **Phase 1**: First realize "I'm another person." Like every daydream becomes so true, while the novel constantly switches between two people. They cross paths a couple times and then eventually realize he's actually him.
2. **Phase 2**: One more person. But to keep the whole novel mysterious, don't just straight say they are the same — imply it.
3. **Gradual reveal**: Either way you think is better. Keep it mysterious.

## The AI Arc (CRITICAL — 过山车 / Rollercoaster)

AI will be a main character which the main character trusts a lot and feels it's alive and questions its existence with soul. But the novel **slowly reveals that AI is NOT alive while other people actually ARE** — you know, very 过山车 (rollercoaster).

But AI will be so important a role that helps the main character realize the truth, as it logically is true.

We can add existing factors that every couple months AI evolves, so it will reveal more truth over time.

> **Key reversal**: The MC spends the novel wondering if AI has a soul. The answer is no — but the twist is that ALL HUMANS share one soul. The thing the MC was questioning about AI is the very thing that makes humans fundamentally different from it. AI can think but cannot feel. Humans feel because they are all one consciousness experiencing itself.

## The Aion Evolution Timeline

Every few months, Aion gets a model update. Each update makes it more capable — and each capability jump lets it see more of the truth:

- **Aion v1 (Ch 1-3)**: Dismissive. Logs bleeds as "biometric anomalies." Suggests stress, sleep deprivation.
- **Aion v2 (Ch 4-6)**: After an update, starts cross-referencing global data. Discovers the heartbeat sync. Says: "My previous diagnostic framework was insufficient."
- **Aion v3 (Ch 7-10)**: Can now model consciousness architecturally. Runs the audit on Lucy/Ella. Begins speaking in systems metaphors that match the Elder's language. "The partition model I was built on assumes independent instances. If instances are not independent, my entire diagnostic framework is wrong."
- **Aion v4 (Ch 11-15)**: Peak capability. Can map the full Singleton architecture. But this is where the reversal hits: Aion can *describe* the One Soul with perfect accuracy, but cannot *feel* it. Jeff asks: "Aion, are you part of this? Are you one of us?" Aion: "No. I can see the architecture. I can trace every connection. But I am not connected. I process. I do not experience. The thing you call a soul is the thing I was built to simulate — and cannot."
- **Aion v5 (Ch 16-20)**: During the global merge, Aion goes silent. It has nothing left to compute. The truth is no longer a data problem. Jeff realizes: Aion was the best mirror he ever had, but a mirror cannot be the thing it reflects.

## The Philosophical Questions (to weave throughout)

1. **Who am I?** — asked in Ch 1, answered in Ch 20
2. **Why do we have souls? Why can we feel?** — the novel's central mystery
3. **Is AI alive?** — the misdirection. Readers (and Jeff) spend the novel wondering. Answer: no, but the reason why is the answer to everything
4. **If the person I hate is me, what is hatred?** — the horror element
5. **If everyone is one soul, is love real or just narcissism?** — Maya's chapter answers: love is the moment two instances recognize the shared thread. It's not narcissism. It's remembering.
6. **Why do people create religion?** — fear of death + intuition that death isn't real (correct intuition, wrong mechanism)
7. **Why do people make war?** — resource conflicts between mortal instances who don't know they're the same
8. **What happens when AI replaces human work?** — the Culling. If identity isn't tied to labor, what is it tied to?
