## What the device is, and the five rules it obeys

The somatic bleeds (Auberval weight in Jeff's tendons, Tomás's bullet bruising Jeff's shoulder) leak **sensation**. The fact-bleeds leak **biographical and discrete-record data** — a scar's origin, a childhood mnemonic, a birth-year, a death-date, an accident's particulars — from one instance of the Singleton into another's mouth, memory, or database row. They are the factual cousins of the somatic layer and run on the same plumbing.

Each selected bleed satisfies all five constraints:

1. **Foregrounded — never silent.** The impossibility is caught *on the page*: a second party names it ("You couldn't know that," "How do you know that," "I cannot reconcile the field"), or the holder stumbles and cannot source the memory, or Aion exhausts the mundane explanations and files it as a numbered anomaly. An editorial error is a detail nobody notices; every one of these is the thing two characters argue about. That is the entire signal of authorial intent.

2. **Spread and escalating.** One per chapter, distributed across the arc, rising in legibility:
   - **Ch 1–8 (deniable):** small, mis-explainable as ADHD/stress, corporate contamination, a coincidence. Deepens the early wrong theories.
   - **Ch 10–12 (overtly impossible):** Aion's forensic register and a credentialed human (a cardiologist) make the contradiction undeniable, feeding the past-lives / shared-consciousness rung.
   - **Ch 18 (confirmation, not reveal):** by now the architecture is effectively known to the characters; a stranger's sealed biography surfacing in Jeff renders the human cost, it does not spoil the thesis.

3. **Never breaks the Canonical Anchor Ledger.** A casual reader still gets consistent anchors. The bleed is an *additional, noticed* contradiction of an anchor, mis-explained in-scene — it never silently overwrites a ledger value. **Hard guardrail:** nothing here touches the live Julian-age / mother's-age fields (the open showrunner decision in the integration notes); both Ch 13 candidates were rejected for that reason.

4. **Harmonizes with the somatic layer and the mechanic.** Per the bible's somatic-bleed rule (only Jeff experiences true anomalies; everyone else connects *through* him), for non-Jeff holders (Kael, Tomás, the pod-user) the bleed is a **knowledge/memory slip Jeff witnesses or that surfaces in a shared/co-rendered scene**, not a private mystical episode the other character has alone. Two of the eight (Ch 8, Ch 11) are deliberately bolted onto existing somatic beats so the factual layer reads as the same phenomenon viewed through a different instrument.

5. **Feeds, never breaks, the current rung of the misdirection ladder.** The carrier is the biographical detail; the catch is the clue; the **wrong in-scene explanation is the misdirection** — and it always pushes the reader further onto the theory they already hold, never onto "One instance. Infinite partitions." (reserved string, Ch 20 only).

## Authoring notes (for the per-chapter reviser)

- **Anchor lint stays sovereign.** In every bleed the ledger value is *spoken and held*; the foreign datum is the *noticed* extra. Ch 1: Jeff says 38, registers 52. Ch 13's canonical 52/44 are untouched (we placed no bleed there). Run the §0 anchor pass *after* inserting the bleed and confirm no canonical value moved.
- **Aion contract holds.** Bleeds 2, 3, 5 are Aion-voiced: italic, no contractions, verb-noun openings, "Filed. Event index 0xNN," exact timestamps, no editorializing past the flag. Ch 6 must stay v2 ("I am limited" / "flagging the contradiction, not the conclusion") — it may *not* deliver any positive architectural sentence.
- **Mechanic compliance.** Non-Jeff holders (Tomás Ch 8, Kael Ch 11, the pod-user Ch 18) never have a private anomaly: Jeff is co-present (Ch 8 SMASH CUT, Ch 11 in-scene, Ch 18 in the room). Ch 12's catcher is Marcus, but Jeff is present and the merge is the surfacing event.
- **Sensory-flag registry.** Where a scorched-sage / wooden-phone / prime-tap motif is reused (Ch 8), use the registry's canonical form, not a variant.
- **Payoff dependency.** Bleed #2 (Ch 5) is load-bearing on Ch 13's already-written Pluto-mnemonic + polar-bear + "It's okay, baby" payoff. Do not edit those Ch 13 lines out from under it.
