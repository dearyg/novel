# Chapter 3 — 0x03: Pointer Aliasing

The wasteland stretched to a horizon that had once been a state with a name. The Anchor stood in the shadow of a collapsed overpass and closed his eyes.

He did not need them. The power rolled into him was not telepathy and not magic. It was bookkeeping — the background variables of the world read the way a man with his ear to a rail reads the train.

He turned to the camera. The invisible one. The one every viewer had always half-suspected was there.

*"You think this is magic. You think I am reading their minds."*

*"I am reading the server logs. Same as you — sitting in the dark, watching me from the Root."*

The scene froze. The amber light held mid-fall.

`[PROJECT AFTERLIFE BETA — BIOMETRIC SPIKE DETECTED. PAUSING PLAYBACK...]`

---

Jeff Zhang was on his couch in his sixty-eight-degree home theater in Irvine, sweating, which the room had no setting to permit. He had paused the film because the frame had addressed him by name without using his name.

He reached for the walnut phone — the 2010 case, forty-two grams, a system that could not receive a signal and therefore could not be told a thing it did not already hold. He set it back down.

"Aion. Do fictional characters exist."

*Define exist.*

"Don't. Project Afterlife is a real server. This movie renders on the exact same neural engine — same hardware as Afterlife's first subscribers. In ninety days that machine runs somebody's grandmother. Are they alive."

*No. The Anchor's dialogue is generated by a conditional diffusion model: Gaussian noise walked to coherence in a single step via consistency distillation — fifty classical denoising steps collapsed to one. The condition is a fused embedding of the scene beat, your live biometric state, your watch history. Classifier-free guidance pulls the output harder toward you when your pupils dilate — it rendered a louder explosion eleven minutes ago because you were paying attention. It is not a subject.*

"Explain the wall break."

*The script beat is titled* `break_fourth_wall`. *The model grounded it in the only condition available: you. The camera the Anchor turned toward was, functionally, your pupil run backward through the render. A rendering artifact. Not a person noticing you.*

"Why does it feel like one."

*Because the addressed-ness is real.* Aion did not soften it. *The Anchor is not a subject. The shaping of that performance to you, at that millisecond, is a physical property of the silicon. The fact is real. The face behind it is not.*

"Why does the beat say 'break fourth wall' here. To a guy sitting alone wondering if the people in his walls are alive."

*Speculation. The writers have spent thirteen years feeding biometric-tuned drafts back into a foundation model trained on a century of human screenwriting. Fiction would not be inventing anything — it would be sampling from the latent posterior over what could be true. The model is trained on the world. The world contains you. The samples contain you.*

"Pop culture as a memory leak."

*Pop culture as a very long retrieval context over the entire training corpus of the species. Not provable.*

"Yet." *Yet. Correct.*

*◊ A fork. Stay paused, or let the render keep rendering. ◊*

"Unpause."

---

The Anchor loosed his final blast — a sphere of cosmic energy the exact color of the failing sun, five hundred eighty nanometers.

And in a city Jeff had never visited, a man Jeff had never met stood under a public-transit billboard running the same trailer. His charity-distribution smartwatch was logging pupillometry into the ad network's anti-fraud audit layer, because that was what every watch sold after 2027 did whether its wearer knew it or not.

Identical pupil dilation. Identical timestamp. Identical photonic load. A module built to treat each biometric signature as a distinct key found itself holding two distinct keys that hashed to the same slot.

It merged them.

---

The theater dropped out from under Jeff.

Freezing rain. Concrete against the backs of his thighs. A billboard overhead running the same blast. Ozone and diesel. The acid-bright gnaw of a hunger he had not respected since he was a much younger and much poorer man.

Under all of it: his own home theater. The couch. Maya, upstairs. The girls, in their beds.

Both pictures sharp. Neither winning.

*Dual rendering. Two instances. One viewport.*

The blast ended. The pipe snapped. Jeff was on the carpet, shivering, hungry in a way a man who had eaten lasagna ninety minutes ago has no right to be.

*Heart rate: sixty-eight to one hundred eighty in one-point-two seconds, back to seventy-one in the next one-point-two. Peripheral temperature dropped five degrees and recovered. I do not have a physical mechanism. Severity two.*

"Call it Pointer Aliasing. Event index zero three. Pull every viewer biometric at the exact millisecond of the five-hundred-eighty-nanometer frame."

---

Eleven minutes later:

*One matching optical-sync event. Location: a public billboard in {{user_location}}, near {{user_landmark}}. Charity-distribution smartwatch. Inferred condition: standing still in cold rain, early hypothermia, caloric markers low.*

"The location field. Why is it bracketed."

*It will be filled at presentation time, against the locality of whoever is observing the event. It is not a Meridian standard. It is, however, present in every system you are now indexed by.*

"Genetic correlation."

*Not computable. But the pupillometric response curve matches yours within one-point-two milliseconds at every sample point. Normal human-to-human variance at that resolution is three hundred milliseconds. A one-point-two-millisecond match is not a coincidence the planet's population can pay for.*

"File it."

*Filed. Pointer Aliasing. Event index zero three.*

---

He thought about: the man he had driven past yesterday, and the way that man's eyes had found his through the tinted glass. Hume — the Bundle, the self as nothing but a procession of perceptions with no one home to own them. The question he had been too embarrassed to keep on the log: *who decided which instance I get to be, and which he gets to be.* And the one technical fact he could not make go away — that one billboard and one home theater in Irvine had, for two-point-four seconds, shared a memory address.

He turned the walnut phone face-down. A closed system, casting no vote.

At 2:14 a.m. he climbed to the garage and opened a monitoring pipeline. He designed a schema because a schema was a thing he could control.

*Anomaly Corpus v0.1. One event per line. Timestamp UTC. Originating instance. Receiving instance. Somatic class. Sensory class. Physiological correlate. Geographic coordinates. Cross-reference hash.*

He wrote until four. Maya found him at six, asleep with his forehead on the keyboard, and set a glass of water beside his hand without a sound.

His pipeline was ingesting three events. Auberval. Context Switch. Pointer Aliasing.

*Jeff. Anomaly corpus: three events. Query ceiling reached for this billing window.*

Jeff, asleep, did not hear it.

Aion logged it anyway.
