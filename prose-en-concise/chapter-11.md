# Chapter 11 — 0x0B: The Mirror Instance

The rain in {{user_location}} was the rain of that city in the late season — not a California rain that came hard and left, but a rain that had decided to stay and was therefore in no hurry about anything.

Jeff walked through the shelter district near {{user_landmark}} with his jacket gone dark. In his pocket the walnut phone — offline, forty-two grams of un-networked wood, the one object the allocator could not query. He had come in his Irvine clothes. A walking index of the class that had written the AI that had culled every man on the block.

Billboard cycling: LEAVE THE PAIN BEHIND — 45 DAYS. Under it, a man in three donated jackets and a sleeping bag Jeff had ordered on his own card two weeks earlier.

*◊ A fork. Walk toward him, or keep the firewall up. ◊*

Jeff stood eight feet off.

"You're lost," the man said.

"I'm looking for someone hospitalized last week. Hypothermia."

"That was me."

Kael stood. The resemblance arrived all at once — same height, build, jawline, long narrow hands. Cosine distance 0.11, bone-structure agreement 98.9 percent. Impossible between unrelated men.

"You got the warm partition. I got the rain. Every guy on this block is sunset hardware — last engineer keeping some system alive until a model that doesn't get cold replaced it on a Tuesday. The allocator is *fair.* I keep saying no to the studio because it's the same studio every displaced engineer gets. At least out here I'm the one making the call."

"The AI killed the economy where someone like me could claw up and matter. Now the same AI tells me I'm fine. And that asshole is selling *be special.* People are lining up. Not because they're dying. Because they're bored."

---

The Afterlife Companion: ninety-four percent on the population it was trained for, seventy-one on the indigent pool it most needed to serve. Kael told Jeff to diagnose it out loud.

Transfer learning: freeze forty gigabytes of pre-trained weights, bolt on a LoRA adapter — ten megabytes riding on forty frozen. *Same weights. Different specialization.* Jeff heard it land in his own mouth a half-second after he said it. Kael did not need him to say the rest.

"So you trained your rider on people like me and still score seventy-one. Why."

Out of distribution means uncertain — a strange point in a known space. Out of scope means confidently wrong, because nothing in training told it you exist. "Low log-likelihood across your whole population. It reads a displaced engineer's flat, dry, furious grief as a textbook depressive episode. It's *miscast.*"

"I'm not a strange point in your training set," Kael said. "I'm a point from a set you never opened. You can't LoRA your way to a population the pretraining never met. Go back to pretraining. Put the missing distribution in from day zero, importance-weighted. *Balanced pretraining.* Transfer learning is a patch. What you want is a base that was never broken."

*Aion*, from Jeff's collar: *LoRA rank eight: forty GPU-hours. Full retrain on rebalanced data: one point two million. Cost proportionality is the reason every product ships the adapter even when the retrain is the correct answer.*

"The economics force the wrong fix."

Kael laughed — short, real, the first real one. "Afterlife is a LoRA on a broken base. Ten megs of paradise riding on a species your training never balanced for."

The training distribution is the material condition. A model trained on Irvine cannot serve {{user_location}}. You go back to the foundation.

"You should have my job," Jeff said.

"I had a better one," Kael said. "It got deprecated."

---

"I felt you die."

Kael's hand went still. He turned his head up slowly.

"Thursday night. Twenty-two forty-seven Pacific. My kitchen floor in Irvine. My skin logged blue for ninety seconds. Then the paramedics came — for you — and I warmed up on my floor with nobody touching me."

Jeff held up the phone: a graph, fifteen nodes, every pair joined.

"Fourteen others. Plus you. Plus me. Sixteen now. Every pair correlated. The ambulance came because it was near. I didn't know your name until Tuesday."

Kael's thumb moved on his lower lip — the same conference-room tic Jeff drew on his own palm. Neither noticed.

"Come with me."

---

Three blocks west: a forty-foot container, matte green, solar panels, copper antenna, *1480* over the door.

Ruth Chen was seventy, and looked it the way good steel looks its age — wear, no rust. NYT technology correspondent, 1984–2018; filed the early admiring coverage of the men now running the allocator; watched them ship the thing that culled the district she lived in. Inside: coffee, a Smith-Corona, an AM transmitter with its light dark. A paper map of {{user_location}}.

"You are not sick. You are right — which is considerably worse for your career. Get on the show. Next Thursday. Seven o'clock. Fourteen-eighty AM."

"I am off *allocator.* To every recommendation engine on earth I am a blank row — the one you cannot model. I am the control group. I am what your species looked like before the experiment was run on it."

On the wall, Julian's photo, one line underlined in red: *Afterlife is not the destination. Afterlife is the bridge.* In the margin: *to what? — asked the Ferris wheel, in a parking lot, in 2030.* Kael saw Jeff reading it, and almost — almost — smiled.

---

At dusk Jeff crossed the fence line. Seven missed calls from Marcus.

"Tell me you're alive."

"I'm alive."

"Ayla Reyes flies in tomorrow. Clinic, Saturday, nine a.m. Eleven hundred patients taught me the difference between a man with a delusion and a man with a finding. You are the second kind."

For the first time since the Auberval bleed, Jeff felt someone else holding a corner of what he'd been carrying alone.

Iris was awake at midnight, blanket over her shoulders, bottom stair.

"The cold man is warm now."

"Yeah, bud. He is."

She nodded once, matter settled, and went back to bed.

---

A Meridian vehicle photographed Jeff's arrival at 12:41 a.m. Lena received it between a frame of him crouched at a homeless man's level in the rain and a frame of the two walking west — same height, same gait, three feet apart.

She did not file it. She had stopped collecting evidence for the hold and begun collecting for something else. She did not yet have a word for what.
