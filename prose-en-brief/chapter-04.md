# Chapter 4 — 0x04: Dual-Stack Rendering

Darius Monroe, forty, walks the tunnel on a knee rebuilt twice. Exo-assist sleeves and AR contacts overlay probabilities on every pocket of the court. Each time he blinks the overlays off the crowd cheers — they paid to watch the thing the AI cannot do.

He dominates the first quarter. In the second, a twenty-three-year-old blows past him; Coach-7 flags thirty-four percent injury probability. Monroe stays in.

At a strip-mall bar in Irvine, Jeff meets Priya Ramanathan — culled when Flexion folded its applied team in 2028, now running red-team gigs. He installs *Aion* v2, deploys a Bayesian change-point detector against his own biometrics, and explains his real job: shrinking Coach-7 from a two-hundred-millisecond server model to AR glasses that must respond in twenty.

The CS concept: **knowledge distillation** (Hinton, 2015) stacked with **quantization**. Train the student on the teacher's probability distribution rather than hard labels — soft targets carry "dark knowledge," the geometry of near-misses. Float32-to-int8 stacks another 4×. Target: 48× compression, ninety-four percent quality. Marcus's Slack: stuck at 30×.

Paired philosophy: **personal identity / Ship of Theseus**. Watching Coach-7 render a perfect 2019 Monroe reel, Monroe asks where he actually lives. His postgame line and *Aion*'s earlier admission rhyme: *I can model what you feel. I cannot feel it.* The missing six percent is the only part that was ever Monroe.

Maya is up when Jeff gets home, reading his errors without naming them. Then his daughter hands him a crayon drawing: a hunched figure on a dark rectangle, a vertical line behind it. *It's cold in there*, she says. The figure matches a hypothermia-grade biometric profile Jeff pulled from an ad network days earlier. He pins it to the fridge. The change-point detector fires. Event index zero four.
