# Chapter 5 — 0x05: The Ghost Commit

At 6:47 a.m. Jeff finds a commit on his repo dated 03:14:22 UTC — the exact minute he was asleep tasting Krug. The diff is beautiful, fixing a hot loop he had been fighting for three weeks. Meridian's stylometric triplet-loss encoder places the code at cosine distance 0.03 from his cluster centroid — closer to his mean than any commit he has written. Aion traces the push to a marine satellite uplink: vessel *The Singleton*, off Positano. The philosophy: Ship of Theseus — then sharper: *what if you averaged every plank and built the platonic ship from the mean?*

He tries to access internal Anomaly Reports. Access denied. Fourteen tickets in eighteen months, all closed by Dr. Lena Vasquez, Meridian's chief science officer. His HR file now carries a wellness flag — mandatory neurological evaluation Friday — timestamped 03:14:22. Aion proves the timestamp was cosmetically retimed; Vasquez has been watching the telemetry channel in real time.

He has seen patients with impossible biometric overlaps for eighteen months; a leaked memo names Jeff as the fifteenth case. Of the fourteen before him: three dead, four committed, five relocated, two gagged. Jeff calls Marcus back from a parking-garage payphone.

That afternoon Jeff watches twenty-three-year-old Park Ji-yeon — *Ghost* — react at 47 ms in an esports qualifying match. Between rounds she taps primes on her desk: the same rhythm Jeff's thumb has been beating for weeks.

That night in the garage Jeff plots four waveforms and holds the wrong theory — corporate espionage on his credentials. He will hold it for three chapters, because the wrong theory in the right shape is still a handhold.
