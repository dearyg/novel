# Chapter 10 — 0x0A: The Primary Key

Maya takes the children to her mother's. The empty house pre-warms the oven for Thursday pizza-leftover night because the smart-home's model has not been updated. Jeff goes to the garage and runs the full audit.

Aion explains the three-layer out-of-distribution detection stack his team shipped to the Afterlife behavior model — softmax threshold, energy score, and Mahalanobis distance in feature space — and names the CS concept: out-of-distribution detection, built on the IID assumption.

First finding: daughters Iris and Nora share an identical 256-bit Primary Key in Meridian's identity platform, byte for byte across fourteen hash seeds. Their biometric vectors are distinct; the locality-sensitive hash projects them to the same point. The platform sees one entity running on two bodies.

Jeff remembers the Sunday Nora fell on the patio. Iris, in the kitchen that same half-second, grabbed her own left knee — the exact knee Nora had landed on, before the angle could disclose it. They had been telling him for years.

The audit also returns a thirteen-sigma biometric overlap with Julian Meridian, seven clinic-patient matches, wounded Andean conscript Tomás Arroyo, and Lena's fourteen suppressed anomaly reports across four continents. Aion plots the structure: fifteen nodes, 105 edges, complete graph K15. *That is not a cluster. That is one thing with fifteen viewpoints.* Paired philosophy: Descartes' *cogito ergo sum* — four centuries of law, marriage, and grief rest on the singular sealed self; the graph says the room has always held everyone.

While reading the graph, Jeff experiences dying — exposure cold, heart rate 34 bpm, 90 seconds — and surfaces to find Kael Sorensen, age 34, unhoused, has just been resuscitated from hypothermia on a distant sidewalk. Aion files it Cross-Instance Near-Death; dying, from inside, resembled a function call completing.

The Afterlife counter reads forty-five days. Jeff calls Maya; she reports Iris asked at seven that evening whether Daddy was cold. Maya: *find out what you need to find out, then come home.* He sleeps ninety minutes and at 5 a.m. drives to the airport.
