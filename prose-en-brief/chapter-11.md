# Chapter 11 — 0x0B: The Mirror Instance

Jeff walks the post-Culling shelter district in {{user_location}} and finds the billboard from his ad-network query. Beneath it, in three layers of donated jackets, sits the man whose hypothermia he experienced from his kitchen floor.

Kael Sorensen is the same height as Jeff, with the same jawline and the same long narrow hands. A facial-recognition pass returns a 98.9% bone-structure match — impossible for unrelated men. Kael, an ex-supply-chain engineer culled with his entire team, refuses the allocator's offered studio unit because refusal is the only thing he still owns. He frames the modern hollowness as a market for *being special* in a room where no one is poor.

Jeff tells him plainly: *I felt you die.* He shows Kael the fifteen-node complete graph rotating on his phone.

Kael walks him three blocks to a forty-foot shipping container in a vacant lot, painted matte green with a hand-painted *1480* over the door. Inside, a seventy-one-year-old journalist named Ruth Chen lives intentionally off the allocator — copper-mesh shielding, paper newspapers, a Smith-Corona, an AM radio transmitter she runs three nights a week.

Ruth tells Jeff he is not sick, he is right, and that being right is worse for his career than being sick would have been. She invites him onto her show. She offers her container as a safe house — it sleeps three; Kael is one, Jeff is the third. Her container has not been indexed by any recommendation engine on Earth. She is, in her own words, the control group.

Walking back, Jeff's phone comes online with seven missed calls from Marcus. Ayla Reyes is flying in. Marcus, Jeff, Ayla, and Kael will meet at the clinic Saturday morning. *That's the whole game,* Marcus says: *you're not alone.*

Jeff lands in Irvine at midnight. Iris is waiting up. *The cold man is warm now*, she tells him, satisfied, and goes back to bed.

A Meridian surveillance vehicle photographs his arrival. Lena receives the photo and files it — but quietly, no escalation. She is collecting evidence for something else now. She does not yet know what.
