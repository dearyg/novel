# Chapter 11 — 0x0B: The Mirror Instance

Jeff walks {{user_location}}'s shelter district on a legitimate assignment — the Afterlife Companion model scores 94% on its training population and 71% on displaced workers — but his real errand is the billboard from his ad-network query. Under it sits Kael Sorensen, former Pacific Routing operations-research engineer, culled when the allocator folded the company in a week. Facial-recognition returns 98.9% bone-structure agreement: same height, same jawline, same long narrow hands — impossible for unrelated men. Kael refuses the allocator's offered studio unit because refusal is the only thing he still owns. He frames the post-Culling hollowness as a market Julian engineered: *Afterlife is a LoRA on a broken base* — ten megs of paradise riding on a species the pretraining never balanced for.

The CS concept is **transfer learning and LoRA**: freeze the 40-gigabyte base, snap on a 10-megabyte adapter. Kael presses the flaw: displaced engineers are not out-of-distribution, they are *out of scope* — a population the pretraining never entered. LoRA cannot conjure knowledge the base never held. The fix is **balanced pretraining** from day zero. *Aion* confirms: LoRA costs 40 GPU-hours; full retrain, 1.2 million. The economics enforce the wrong answer every time. Paired philosophy: the training distribution is the material condition — no adapter repairs a foundation built without you.

Jeff tells Kael *I felt you die* and shows the fifteen-node complete graph. Kael walks him to **Ruth Chen**, 70 — former NYT tech correspondent, off-allocator by design: copper-mesh container, Smith-Corona 1974, AM transmitter 1480 kHz. In her own words, the control group: the blank row no model can profile. She tells Jeff he is not sick, he is *right*, which is worse for his career. She offers the container as a safe house and an on-air slot Thursday.

Exiting, Jeff's phone wakes: seven missed calls from Marcus. Ayla flies in tomorrow; the group meets at the clinic Saturday. *You are not alone.*

Midnight: Iris waits on the bottom stair. *The cold man is warm now,* she says, and goes to bed.

Lena receives surveillance photos — Jeff at Kael's level, same height, same gait. The statutory hold would clear in an afternoon. She does not file it. She closes the laptop in the dark and begins collecting evidence for something else. She does not yet have a word for what.
