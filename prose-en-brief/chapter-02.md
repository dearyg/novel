# Chapter 2 — 0x02: Priority Inversion

Julian Meridian, fifty-six, founder and CEO of Meridian Industries, sits on his three-hundred-foot yacht *The Singleton* off the Amalfi Coast.

His morning begins with the absence of friction — light tuned to his biological peak, an Auberval Astralis on his wrist, a hologram briefing him on a priority-inversion bug in the Afterlife scheduler. He approves a fix and quietly defies a board push to monetize Premium Empathy Packages.

He learns that Hari Patel — the board member he forced out three years earlier over Afterlife ethics, now terminally ill — has accumulated eleven percent of Meridian stock and intends a hostile pause. Julian capitulates by proposing a public ethics consultation Hari himself will chair.

By private routine, he drops into the live feed of an engineering sprint at his Irvine subsidiary. A saliency model flags one fidgeting employee — Jeff Zhang. Julian murmurs *"nice phone"* to no one; the Concierge interprets it as a command and routes a micro-ping into Jeff's earpiece.

Then Julian's reality fractures. He smells Scorched Sage. He tastes day-old coffee. The Auberval is gone; in his hand is a forty-two-gram walnut phone he has never owned. He whispers, *"Someone was just me."* The episode lasts forty-one seconds.

Three thousand miles away, Jeff wakes at 3:14 a.m. tasting Krug. Aion correlates the two events at the same UTC second. The vessel-redacted log lists the source: *THE SINGLETON*. Aion files the new class as Context Switch.

For the first time in nine years, Julian's longest-running question — *if you have abolished all the friction, is there still anything there?* — has an answer. There is something. It is somebody else.
