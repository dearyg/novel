# Chapter 2 — 0x02: Priority Inversion

Julian Meridian, fifty-two, founder and CEO of Meridian Industries, sits on his three-hundred-foot yacht *The Singleton* off the Amalfi Coast. His mornings run frictionless — light tuned to his biological peak, nootropics delivered by implant, an Auberval Astralis watch he set himself as the one analog object he trusts.

The Concierge briefs him on a **priority-inversion** bug in the Afterlife beta scheduler: a low-tier breathing simulation holds a shared audit-log lock; a high-tier sensory pass stalls behind it; a mid-tier ad-delivery process runs ahead of both. The inversion mirrors the 1997 Mars Pathfinder fault. Julian approves bounded priority inheritance (four-millisecond ceiling), commissions a write-up that effectively builds the case for full lock removal, and declines to sell the soul as a subscription — his cortisol spikes at the board's "Premium Empathy Packages" proposal, not at the technical name for it.

He learns that Hari Patel — ousted from the board in 2027 over Afterlife ethics, now terminally ill with metastatic prostate cancer — has accumulated eleven percent of Meridian stock. Julian responds at aggression-scale one: he concedes publicly, proposes a ninety-day ethics consultation Hari himself will chair, and withdraws every legal countermeasure. His cortisol returns to baseline.

By private routine, Julian drops into an Irvine sprint feed. A saliency model flags Jeff Zhang. Julian murmurs *"nice phone"*; the Concierge routes it as a live micro-ping into Jeff's earpiece.

Then Julian's reality fractures. He smells Scorched Sage. He tastes day-old coffee. The Auberval is gone; in his hand is a forty-two-gram walnut phone he has never owned. The episode lasts forty-one seconds. He says, quietly, *"Someone was just me."*

Three thousand miles west, Jeff wakes at 3:14 a.m. tasting Krug. *Aion* overlays the two biometric traces at the identical UTC second and names the new event class **Context Switch** (index 02). The vessel designation in the log is unredacted: *THE SINGLETON*.

For the first time in nine years, Julian's longest open question — *if you have abolished all the friction, is there still anything there?* — has its first answer. There is something. It is somebody else.
