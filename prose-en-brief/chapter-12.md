# Chapter 12 — 0x0C: The Three-Body Problem

Saturday morning at Marcus Meridian's clinic, four people pool independent datasets: Jeff's anomaly corpus, Marcus's eleven clinical patients, Ayla Reyes's leaked Meridian Aerospace memos, and Kael's eleven-year analog logistics notebook.

Aion runs a cross-correlation lag-finder, aligns the three clocks against known co-occurrences, and merges everything onto a single time axis. Forty-one anomalous overlap events. The geographic pattern is non-random — clusters around seven US cities plus Lagos, Osaka, São Paulo. The correlations predate Afterlife by a decade.

Marcus then puts a card on the table. He had written his hypothesis nine weeks earlier, before he ever called Jeff: that the boundary between two patients is, at the layer his biometrics measure, a configuration, not a fact. He had withheld it deliberately so Jeff's data would not bend toward his voice. The two datasets agree from independent draws. *That is replication.*

Ayla relays that Julian himself authorized her to bring the Meridian Aerospace material to "any engineering team" investigating Afterlife's safety architecture. He is waiting to see what they find before he decides.

At noon they play pickup basketball. Aion writes a falsifiable prediction to log first: *Cross-instance motor-sync between you and Kael in the next ninety seconds.* Forty-one seconds later Jeff makes a no-look pass and Kael catches without looking — motor planning aligned at 2.3 milliseconds prior to contact, below conscious reaction time. The fact that Aion *called* it before it happened is the upgrade.

At 2:14 p.m., three Meridian Systems security uniforms walk in with a compliance warrant and seize a subset of Marcus's files. Aion has already distributed the merged corpus to three Byzantine-resistant nodes — Jeff's garage, Ayla's encrypted orbital backup, and a paper copy couriered to Ruth's mail slot. Kael is the fourth node; his notebook cannot be seized electronically.

In the parking lot Ayla delivers Julian's private invitation: come to Geneva on the fifteenth. *I am ready to see the whole of what I built.* She also tells Jeff, on her own behalf, that Afterlife can only be stopped by Julian — he owns the infrastructure, the board is already past him, he is the last lever.

That night Jeff sleeps seven hours on the floor of Ruth's container under a wool blanket and dreams of a man on a plane looking down at a yacht through a cloud break. He will not know he had dreamed this until he is doing it, three chapters from now.
