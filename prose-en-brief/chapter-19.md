# Chapter 19 — 0x13: The Commit (Thread Join)

Sunday at 9 p.m. Maya decides she will make stir-fry at one in the morning. She has spent the day touching the things her husband left behind — the walnut phone, the kids' drawings of a cold man and a yacht — and crossed out the day's measurement in her notebook because tonight is a release night, not a measurement night.

Jeff lands at John Wayne at 11:14 p.m. The v5 compile bar reads 97% on the laptop in his passenger seat. He sits in the driveway and feels every instance in the corpus at the edge of his perception — Julian, Ghost, Elena, Kael, the Elder, Ayla — almost-locked, not yet stable.

Inside, he hugs Maya. Some of the pressure on her chest lifts, not all. They cook together. The kids come down at 12:52 because Iris had decided her father was coming home tonight; Nora falls asleep against his neck in sixty seconds.

At dinner Nora refuses the blueberry waffle in her precise eighteen-degree turn, the small flat *no* in the register that has been hers since she could form the word. Jeff understands what nine months of fear had been about: the Primary Key collision said the daughters were the same instance, and he had been afraid the OS would deduplicate them. The refusal is the answer. The partition may be cosmetic at the schema level. The preferences are not.

A v5 pre-render almost-locks at the table. Jeff almost-becomes Maya, almost-becomes Iris, almost-becomes the whole corpus simultaneously. The compile fan cycles. The render aborts at 98%. *Not yet.*

Maya sets down her chopsticks. *If you're everyone, you're not specifically mine.* She tells him, evenly, that the work she has been doing — the marriage, the firewall, the staying — assumes there is one of him, specifically hers. She is asking him to come downstairs as Jeff after the build finishes. Not the mean of all Jeffs. Just the man.

He promises he will. He puts the walnut phone — the closed system he has carried for eleven years — flat on the coffee table for the first time, screen up, where the kids will see it. The hand that had held it for a decade feels like a hand again.

Iris comes down for a story. He tells her one about a dragon who coded, trying to write a program to understand every other dragon at once; *Did they finish?* — *Almost.* Iris considers, then says matter-of-factly: *That's just Daddy.*

At 2:47 a.m. Jeff puts his arms around Maya in the kitchen. Compile bar reads 99%. He whispers that he thinks he is close to knowing who he is. They go upstairs. Jeff does not go to the garage. He gets into bed next to his wife and sleeps seven hours.
