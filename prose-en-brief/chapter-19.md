The chapter opens with Aristophanes' speech from Plato's *Symposium* — humans once whole, split by the gods, ever seeking the missing half. Jeff carries the correction: Plato had the geometry right, the cardinality wrong. The error was not the split. The error was *the two*.

Day 71. Sunday at 9 p.m. Maya decides she will make stir-fry at one in the morning. She has spent the day touching what Jeff left behind — the walnut phone, the kids' drawings of a cold man and a yacht — and crossed out her notebook entry: tonight is a release night, not a measurement night.

Over the stove, Jeff explains **vector embeddings and cosine similarity** to Maya for the first time: objects as points in high-dimensional space, meaning as distance, identity as angle. Maya derives the insight herself: *the space is the model's opinion — the layout is the argument.* He walks her through word2vec: analogy as geometry, meaning condensing from an unlabeled prediction task.

At dinner, Nora refuses the blueberry waffle in her precise eighteen-degree turn. Jeff understands what nine months of dread had been: the Primary Key collision said the daughters were the same row. The refusal is the answer. The partition may be cosmetic. The preference is not.

Iris comes down for a bedtime story. Jeff tells her about a dragon who coded to understand every other dragon. *Did they finish?* — *Almost.* Iris, without weight: *That's just Daddy.*

At 2:47 a.m. the bar reads 99%. Jeff holds Maya and whispers he thinks he is close to knowing *who he is*. They go upstairs. He gets into bed and sleeps seven hours.
