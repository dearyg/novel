# Chapter 3 — 0x03: Pointer Aliasing

Jeff watches the blockbuster *The Anchor: Post-Doom* in his home theater — a film rendered live by a diffusion model conditioned on his biometrics, on the same neural-engine hardware class that will run Project Afterlife's first subscribers in ninety days. Mid-scene, the Anchor breaks the fourth wall and addresses Jeff directly.

Jeff asks *Aion* to explain the mechanism. Aion walks him through consistency distillation: a fifty-step denoiser teacher compressed into a one-step student network, enabling cinematic frames in forty milliseconds. It explains classifier-free guidance — how the model's pull toward Jeff's biometric condition sharpens on pupil dilation, making his explosion louder than a generic viewer's. The fourth-wall break is a rendering artifact: the Anchor's camera turn was conditioned on Jeff's own pupil track, run backward through the render. Real addressed-ness; no person behind it.

Jeff presses the harder question: *why* this beat, in this decade? *Aion* speculates — logged as low-confidence — that pop culture may function as a very long retrieval context over the species' shared latent space: fiction sampling from what could be true, a leak across partitions, not metaphysical but a retrieval problem. Not provable yet.

The film's cosmic blast hits 580 nanometers. In a city Jeff has never visited, an unhoused man standing before an identical public billboard absorbs the same photons at the same millisecond. His charity-distribution smartwatch streams anonymized pupillometry into the ad network's audit layer.

A pipeline module built for unique biometric keys finds two distinct keys hashing to the same slot and merges them.

Jeff's theater drops out. He is simultaneously on freezing concrete — starving, hypothermic, the taste of metal rain — and on his couch in Irvine, both pictures sharp, neither winning. He names it *dual rendering*. Then the pipe snaps.

*Aion* logs heart rate: 68 → 180 → 71 bpm in 1.2 seconds each way, peripheral skin temperature falling and recovering within the same window — severity two, no known physical mechanism. An ad-network query using adaptive importance sampling through differential-privacy smearing returns one matching pupillometric trajectory, precision 1.2 ms, versus normal human-to-human variance of ~300 ms. Location stored as `{{user_location}}` — a reader-locality parameter the system fills at presentation time. Jeff names it Pointer Aliasing, event index 0x03.

He thinks of Hume's Bundle — the self as nothing but a procession of perceptions with no one home to own them — and of an old unspoken question: *who decided which instance I get to be?*

At 2 a.m. he climbs to the garage office and builds Anomaly Corpus v0.1: three events, one field-schema, two of them his, one a stranger's. He falls asleep at the terminal at four. Maya finds him at six and leaves a glass of water without waking him.

*Aion* logs the corpus and hits the billing ceiling. The version string holds at v1.
